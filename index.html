    I am an experimental psychology researcher with a strong focus on cognitive neuroscience, memory, and the impact of real-world factors like stress, anxiety, sleep, and impulsivity. I hold an M.S. in Experimental Psychology from Eastern Kentucky University, where I led EEG-based studies and presented original research at regional conferences including SEPA and KPA.
    With experience as both a research and teaching assistant, I’ve contributed to advanced work in psychophysiology labs, facilitated undergraduate learning, and supported faculty-driven academic research. I bring technical expertise in MATLAB, EEGLAB, AcqKnowledge, and Qualtrics, and I’m fluent in both English and Lingala. As a member of the Psi Chi Honor Society, I’m dedicated to advancing psychological science and fostering academic excellence.
